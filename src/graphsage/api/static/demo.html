DeepSentinel — Network Fraud Analysis

Enter a transaction (or pick an example) and the graph model checks the money network around it: who sent funds to whom, whether senders are brand-new accounts, and whether the receiving account behaves like a collection point for stolen money (a “mule”).

How do I read this picture?
Circles are bank accounts, arrows are money moving between them.
Bigger circles moved money more often. Thicker arrows are transactions the model paid more attention to. The dashed arrow is the transaction you submitted.
Why do I see other accounts, not just my two?
Fraud rings only become visible in context: the model walks two steps outward from your transaction and shows everyone connected to it.
What is a “suspected mule”?
An account that collects money from many (often brand-new) senders and empties out fast — the classic cash-collection point of organized fraud.
